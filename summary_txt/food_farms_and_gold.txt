FOOD, FARMS, GOLD, AND TOWER RENDER OFFSET
=========================================

Purpose
-------
This document explains the economy and presentation pass that added:
- a downward render offset for arrow tower sprites
- growth time for planted trees
- harvestable gold deposits
- a proper farm structure that grows crops around itself
- food upkeep that powers structures over time

The goal of this pass is to make the economy more interdependent.
Towers and production buildings no longer operate forever for free.
Food now matters, farms are the source of that food, and gold now exists as a
real world resource instead of only a UI counter.


1. Arrow tower render offset
----------------------------
Problem:
The tower art includes transparent space near the front of the sprite, which
made the visible tower appear too high relative to the ground.

Current solution:
- arrow towers now draw 24 pixels lower than their collision footprint
- collision, blocking, and placement stay at the original world position
- selection and occlusion use the visible sprite rect, so visual interaction
  still matches what is on screen

Important detail:
This is only a render offset.
It does not move the collision rectangle, does not change path blocking, and
does not change where projectiles originate from in gameplay space.

Implementation area:
- world_objects.py
- STRUCTURE_RENDER_OFFSETS
- Structure._sprite_draw_rect(...)
- Structure.get_sprite_world_rect(...)


2. Trees now take time to grow
------------------------------
Problem:
Lumberyard replanting created fully grown trees immediately, which made the
replant loop look unnatural and let the wood supply recover too quickly.

Current solution:
- planted trees begin in an immature state
- immature trees are visible but smaller and not harvestable
- each planted tree grows over a timed maturation period
- once growth finishes, it becomes a normal harvest target

Current behavior:
- naturally spawned world trees begin fully grown
- only planted trees use the growth timer

Implementation area:
- world_objects.py
- ResourceNodeDefinition.growth_duration
- ResourceNode.growth_elapsed
- ResourceNode.is_harvestable
- ResourceNode.draw(...)

Gameplay impact:
- replanting still sustains wood income
- the player cannot instantly convert planted saplings into wood
- farmed forests now feel like renewable resources rather than instant spawns


3. Gold deposits
----------------
Problem:
Gold existed in the resource UI and upgrade costs, but there was no actual
world source to gather more of it.

Current solution:
- gold deposits now spawn as a harvestable world resource
- gold deposits can be gathered with the existing right-click harvest flow
- additional gold nodes are also seeded near the base so the player is not
  forced to search the entire map before gold becomes relevant

Implementation area:
- world_objects.py
- RESOURCE_DEFINITIONS["gold"]
- RESOURCE_TERRAIN_RULES["gold"]
- WorldObjectManager._spawn_resource_nodes(...)
- WorldObjectManager._seed_resources_near_base(...)

Gameplay impact:
- gold is now a real economy input
- higher tower upgrades that cost gold are supported by the world simulation


4. Farm building
----------------
Requirement:
The farm uses the same base structure sprite as the lumberyard, but should grow
farmland around itself.

Current solution:
- the farm now uses the lumberyard building sprite as its base art
- the structure draws multiple crop plots around the building footprint
- crop plots advance through a growth cycle over time
- when a plot finishes growing, it yields food to the player and restarts its
  growth cycle

Visual model:
- the building itself remains the tall central structure
- crop plots sit lower on the ground around the front area of the farm
- crop colors shift from greener growing crops to more golden ready crops

Implementation area:
- world_objects.py
- BUILDING_SPRITE_PATHS["farm"]
- Structure._create_farm_plots(...)
- Structure._update_farm(...)
- Structure._draw_farm_growth(...)

Gameplay impact:
- farms now visibly produce something over time
- food income is no longer abstract; it is tied to visible growth around the
  structure


5. Food upkeep powers structures
--------------------------------
Problem:
Food was tracked in the UI but had no gameplay consequence.
That made farms optional and left towers permanently free to operate.

Current solution:
- operational structures now consume food on a fixed interval
- if the player has enough food, upkeep is paid and the structure stays active
- if upkeep cannot be paid, the structure becomes inactive until food is
  available again

Current rules:
- arrow towers consume food and will stop firing when starved
- lumberyards and other staffed/functional buildings also consume food over
  time
- farms do not consume food, because they are the recovery path for the food
  economy
- walls and traps remain exempt because they are not staffed utility buildings

Implementation area:
- world_objects.py
- BuildDefinition.food_upkeep
- Structure._update_food_upkeep(...)
- WorldObjectManager._update_towers(...)

Why farms are exempt:
If farms also required food to operate, the player could enter a full deadlock
state at zero food with no way to recover.
Leaving farms as the source structure prevents the economy from bricking itself
while still making food a real limiting factor for expansion.


6. Sidebar structure status
---------------------------
The right-hand panel now exposes the new economy state when a structure is
selected.

Current examples:
- arrow tower shows combat stats plus food upkeep status
- farm shows produced food total and ready plot count
- lumberyard shows delivered wood plus food upkeep state

Implementation area:
- gameui.py

Reason:
Food-based shutdowns need to be visible to the player, otherwise a tower that
stops firing would look like a bug instead of a supply problem.


7. Mental model for the new economy
-----------------------------------
The current intended loop is:
1. place a farm to establish steady food income
2. place operational structures such as towers and lumberyards
3. let those structures spend food over time to stay active
4. expand farms as the base grows and upkeep increases
5. harvest gold deposits to support higher-end upgrades

This makes the economy less flat.
Wood and stone cover construction, gold supports advanced spending, and food now
acts as the operating budget that keeps the settlement alive.


8. Extension points
-------------------
Natural follow-up improvements from this version:
1. add explicit food-upkeep totals for the whole base in the right-hand panel
2. add farm upgrade levels that increase plot count or growth speed
3. add distinct gold deposit sprites if dedicated asset art becomes available
4. add a visible starving or offline badge over inactive structures in the
   world view
5. let enemies prioritize farms once food becomes strategically important
6. add separate crop seasons or terrain bonuses for farm placement
